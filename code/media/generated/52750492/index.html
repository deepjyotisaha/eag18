The Law of Conservation of Momentum

A Visual Presentation for Middle School Physics

Elastic Collision in 1D

Demonstration of two balls bouncing off each other, conserving both momentum and kinetic energy.

Inelastic Collision in 1D

Demonstration of two objects sticking together after the collision, conserving momentum but not kinetic energy.

2D Collision

Demonstration of collisions in two dimensions with varying masses and angles.

Newton's Cradle

Demonstration of momentum transfer in a series of swinging balls.

Bouncing Balls

Demonstration of bouncing balls with adjustable mass.

Carts on a Frictionless Track

Demonstration of carts colliding with spring bumpers, illustrating momentum conservation.

Conclusion: Conservation of Momentum

In all of these demonstrations, the total momentum of the system remains constant, provided no external forces act on it. This is the Law of Conservation of Momentum!
